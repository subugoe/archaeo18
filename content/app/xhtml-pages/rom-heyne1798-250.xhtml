In the Religion itself of the Etruscans, as far as it can be traced, there is the oriental Pantheism,
the Dualism, the Tina (the primary emanation from the Ineffable Supreme), with his
twelve Dii Consentes, half male & half female, (twelve was the sacred & dominant num-
ber, not in Egypt & Etruria alone, but in Palestine, in Asia Minor, & other parts of the
East,) the Cupra or the Juno; the Minerva, the Neith of Egypt, & the Athena of Greece;
the Sethlans, the Vulcan, or the Pthah; still there is nothing which indicates a pe-
culiar relationship to Egypt than to any other part of the East.
On the remarkable coincidences in the style of building - the discovery of Egyptian anti-
quities in the very oldest sepulchres,- the extraordinary resemblance ot the sepulchres
themselves with the necropolis of Thebes - the apparent analogies between the Etrus-
can & the Egyptian mythology, particularly in the ceremonies of the dead - (the Mantxx
of Etruria answering in his office, as well as closely corresponding in his name, with the
Amenti of Egypt) - on these grounds Signor Micali does not hesitate to rest his hy-
pothesis of the civilazation of Etruria by a sacerdotal colony from Egypt.
