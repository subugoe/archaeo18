The unfinished bust of Brutus (cf Matthews's Diary) at Florence by M. Angelo is inscribed
"Dum Bruti efficiem Sculptor de marmore ducit,
In mentem sceleris venit, et abstinuit".
As Brutus' form the sculptors' fancy plann'd,
He recollects the crime, & checks his hand.
The late Lord Sandwich on reading these lines, & mistaking it for the Bust of Marcus Brutus, immediately
wrote the following verses.
"Brutum effecisset sculptor, sed mente recursat
Tanti viri virtus, sistit et abstinuit":
Though Brutus' form the Sculptor's art began,
He stopp'd - nor could achieve the godlike man."
