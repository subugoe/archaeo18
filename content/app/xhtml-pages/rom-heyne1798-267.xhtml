On the unfinished bust of Michael Angelo's Brutus is the following Inscription (cf. Matthews Diary)
"Dum Bruti effigiem Sculptor de marmore duxit,
In mentem sceleris venit, et abstinuit."
As Brutus' form the sculptor's fancy plann'd,
He recollects the crime, and checks his hand.
The late Lord Sandwich, on reading these lines, & conceiving the bust to be that
of M. I. Brutus, immediately wrote the following verses:
"Brutum effecisset Sculptor, sed mente recursat
Tanta viri virtus, sistit & abstinuit."
"Though Brutus's form the Sculptor's art began,
He stopp'd - nor could atchieve the godlike man"
