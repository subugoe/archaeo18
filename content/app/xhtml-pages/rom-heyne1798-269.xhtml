Michael Angelo's unfinished Brutus has all the air of a blacksmith. By the
way, this is not intended, as it has been often supposed, for Marcus Brutus. It is a
portrait of one of the Medici, who assassinated his uncle, & was called the Florentine
Brutus; but proving afterwards the oppressor, and not the liberator, of his
country, M. Angelo laid aside his unfinished bust in disgust.

Mammertine Prison.
Nothing can show the difference between the antient & modern systems of government more
strongly, than the limited size of this prison, compared with the innumerable jails that
now abound in every quarter of Europe: - & yet this was the only prison in old Rome:
"Sub regibus atque tribunis
Viderunt uno contentam carcere Romam."
The habeas corpus bill becomes, indeed, an object of importance, when the prisons of a King
dom contain accommodations for thousands of its inhabitants. Matthews. - Lentulus
was here confined: cod.
Coaches in Rome
Only women were indulged with the use of carriages.
Carpentum, was an open carriage, without cushions or springs.
Towards the 4.th century ab U. C., when the Republic was in want of money, those ladies
who delivered their gold & jewels into the treasury, obtained the privilege to ride in a
pilentum, a covered carriage.
The Carruca (Carosse) which seems to resemble our coach, is an invention
of a much later date. It came into use under the Emperors only. Pliny is
the first who mentions them.-
