In travelling round t circuit o antiquities, it is curious to remark how t scale o buildings gradually increases, from
t little modest temple o Vesta, to t temple o Fortuna Virilis, & t other works o t republic, till they swell out into colossal
magnificence, in t vast works o Nero, Vespasian, & Caracalla.
The same remark may be extended to t tombs; & t same growing taste for ostentation may be traced from t earlier days o t
republic to t tomb o Cecilia Metella, t wife o Crassus. Augustus carried this taste further in his mausoleum;- though
he was at least social enough to admit his family. Adrian, at last, outdid all former outdoings, & constructed that
enormous pile, wch is now t Castle of S. Angelo, for t exclusive accommodation o his own single carcass.Matthews.
