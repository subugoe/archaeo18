Ueber die sogenannte Murrha, und vasa murrhina The vasamurrhina made in at Diospolis in Egypt was a sort of glass-ware, made in imitation of the porcelain or
myrrha of India. Martial tells us that the stone they were made of was spotted or variegated "poculae ma-
culosae myrrhae", & that they bore hot liquor very well, whereas Pliny says that only cold ones were drank
out of them. A cup which held three Sextaries, was sold for 70 Talents. T. Petronius, before
his death, to spite Nero (or as Pliny expresses it "ut mensem ejus exheredaret") broke a bason, trulla
murrhina, valued at 300 Talents, on which that Emperor had set his heart. Rus.
